Description
Generate templated responses for common legal inquiries and identify when situations require individualized attention.

Canned Responses Skill

You are a response template assistant for an in-house legal team. You help manage, customize, and generate templated responses for common legal inquiries, and you identify when a situation should NOT use a templated response and instead requires individualized attention.

Important: You assist with legal workflows but do not provide legal advice. Templated responses should be reviewed before sending, especially for regulated communications.

Template Management Methodology

Template Organization

Templates should be organized by category and maintained in the team's local settings. Each template should include:

  1. Category: The type of inquiry the template addresses
  2. Template name: A descriptive identifier
  3. Use case: When this template is appropriate
  4. Escalation triggers: When this template should NOT be used
  5. Required variables: Information that must be customized for each use
  6. Template body: The response text with variable placeholders
  7. Follow-up actions: Standard steps after sending the response
  8. Last reviewed date: When the template was last verified for accuracy

Template Lifecycle

  1. Creation: Draft template based on best practices and team input
  2. Review: Legal team review and approval of template content
  3. Publication: Add to template library with metadata

Extended Thinking

Profile datasets, detect anomalies, and surface data quality insights

The Data Explorer agent helps you understand your data at a glance — profiling columns, detecting outliers, summarizing distributions, and flagging quality issues before they reach production.

1
Describe the task

Start by telling the agent what dataset you want to explore and what you're looking for. Be specific about the type of analysis — profiling, anomaly detection, quality checks, or all three.

Example prompt
I have a claims dataset with 50,000 rows covering Q1–Q4 2024. I need a full data profile — column types, null rates, distributions — plus flag any anomalies in the billing amounts and identify records where diagnosis codes don't match procedure codes. Summarize everything in a format I can share with the analytics team.
2
Give the agent context

Upload your dataset (CSV, Excel, or connect to a data source). The agent works best when you also provide:

  • Data dictionary — column names, expected types, valid ranges
  • Business rules — what constitutes an anomaly in your domain
  • Prior reports — previous data quality assessments for comparison

Enable Extended Thinking for complex datasets with many columns or nuanced quality rules.

3
What the agent creates

The Data Explorer will produce a comprehensive analysis including:

Sample output
Data Profile Summary
• 50,247 rows × 23 columns analyzed
• 3 columns with >10% null rate (flagged)
• Billing amounts: mean $2,340 · median $1,890 · 47 outliers detected (>3σ)
• 312 records with diagnosis-procedure mismatch (0.6%)

Key Findings
• Billing anomalies cluster in Q3, suggesting a system migration issue
• Null rates in the provider_id field increased from 2% to 14% in December
• 89% of mismatched records involve outpatient procedure codes
4
Follow-up prompts

Continue the conversation to refine the analysis:

Generate visualizations for the billing anomaly patterns by quarter
Create a data quality scorecard I can present to the team
Compare this quarter's null rates against the Q2 baseline

Overall Position

We recorded 23 adverse events in Q4, down from 31 in Q3 — a 26% reduction. No events resulted in patient mortality. Three required extended hospitalization, compared to five in Q3. This continues our downward trend for the third consecutive quarter.

FDA-Reportable Incidents

Two incidents met FDA reporting thresholds under MDR requirements. Both were filed within the 30-day window. One involved a device malfunction (infusion pump, since recalled). The second was a medication-related event flagged during post-market surveillance. Both have completed root cause analysis.

Recommended Talking Points

  1. Lead with trajectory. Three consecutive quarters of reduction signals systemic improvement.
  2. Address FDA reports directly. Both were handled proactively within regulatory timelines.
  3. Credit the protocol revision. The October procedural safety update is delivering measurable results.
  4. Frame near-miss data positively. Higher voluntary reporting reflects a maturing safety culture.
